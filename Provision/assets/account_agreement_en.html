1. How we collect and process your personal information

1.1 Registration and login

a. When you register, log in to the system account, bind a device, or retrieve your account password, we collect the necessary information you provide to create or verify the account, including your mobile number, system account name, and password. You may also choose to supplement other account information (e.g., nickname, avatar) for personalized display. The above information is used solely for system account identification, display, and authentication.

b. Unless otherwise specified, Doubao’s associated products use a unified account service. The system account you register serves as a unified credential, enabling synchronized login and use of Doubao and its associated products without separate re-registration. The binding between this system account and third-party accounts, and account penalties, are also linked under the unified account service. See Learn about the unified account service system for the scope of associated products.

1.2 Cloud services

a. Data sync

After you enable Cloud Services, you can choose whether to sync specific types of local data (such as Contacts/Address Book, Notes, and Browser). Each data sync requires your authorization and can be disabled at any time in system settings. Once enabled, we will sync the following data according to your settings:

Contacts/Address Book data (such as contact name, mobile number, email, nickname, notes, avatar, and other contact information you actively provide)

Notes data (such as text, images, audio, and other note content you actively edit)

Browser data (such as browser bookmarks)

We use encryption technologies, security protections, and access control mechanisms not lower than industry standards to protect your cloud data from unauthorized access and malicious attacks and conduct regular security audits to maintain data integrity and confidentiality.

b. Cloud management

You can view your cloud storage usage or manage your cloud data via [Settings > System Account > Cloud Services]. To realize these functions, we collect and display statistics such as total storage capacity, used and remaining space, and storage distribution.

1.3 Find Device

a. Enable/disable service

You can enable or disable Find Device at any time in [Settings > System Account > Find Device]. When enabling or disabling, you need to enter your system account and password for identity verification.

b. Device location and control

After enabling Find Device, if the device is lost, you can visit [https://find.obriccloud.com/web/find] to remotely locate, lock, or erase device data.

To realize these functions, we may need to collect your device information (unique device identifiers IMEI/IMSI), location information (GPS positioning), and Bluetooth status information (such as Bluetooth name) to perform device location and send control commands to the device.

1.4 Operational security and assurance

During registration, login, or use of related services, to ensure account security, service stability, and system operation security, we may collect your device, network, and log information for identity verification, risk identification, security protection, and operational status analysis.

2. System sensitive permissions we invoke

We only request permissions necessary to provide the System Account service, and no permissions are enabled by default or forced. You can enable or disable each System Account service at any time from [Settings > System Account].

Cloud Services: To implement file sync, backup, and cross-device access, we connect to the network when you enable Cloud Services to sync your contacts, notes, and browser data, and we request the following permissions during use: read and write media data (such as photos, videos, and audio), and read and edit the address book.

Find Device: To help you locate, lock, and protect a lost device, the device can be located, locked, or erased to protect device data and privacy. During use, we will connect to the network and request the following permissions: Bluetooth connection, reading unique device identifiers (such as IMSI/IMEI), reading the user’s phone number, and reading location information.

System Account: This app provides account registration, login, and management services. To ensure proper operation, the System Account and underlying services will connect to the network and request the following permissions: camera, read and write media data (such as photos, videos, and audio), and read SMS.

4. Managing your personal information

4.1 View/correct/supplement account information

Operate via [Settings > System Account > Personal Information] to view or modify your avatar, nickname, and other system account information.

4.2 Change or withdraw authorization scope

On [Settings > System Account], you can enable or disable services provided by the System Account at any time, including Cloud Services and Find Device. We will stop processing related data after you disable the corresponding function, without affecting processing previously conducted based on your authorization.

4.3 Cancel the system account

You can apply to cancel the "Doubao Unified Account" via the Doubao platform (see Account Cancellation Notice). After cancellation, we will cease providing System Account services and other services of associated products that use the Doubao Unified Account for login.

You acknowledge and understand that canceling the System Account is irreversible. After cancellation, we will cease providing any System Account services and will delete all information related to your System Account or anonymize relevant information. If laws and regulations require retention of personal information, we commit to storing it separately and not using such information in daily business activities.
